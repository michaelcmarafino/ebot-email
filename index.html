Vehicle Guidance

The vehicle will utilize laser guidance technology for routing throughout the facility. A rotating laser scanner detects stationary mounted reflectors and measures distance and angle to accurately determine the vehicle position.

These coordinates are used in conjunction with the vehicle odometry information (speed, angle and direction as measured with encoders) to accurately calculate the exact vehicle position as they navigate throughout the system.

Targets are a simple, retro reflective, tape cylinder that is mounted on walls and columns throughout the layout. These targets are installed and surveyed to define a specific x/y coordinate.

The Vehicle Control Computer monitors and controls all vehicle functions including inputs and outputs to motor controllers, the guidance sensors, on-board safety systems, the lift sub-system, and all the data transmitted from the vehicle to the wireless network.

Battery Charging

We will provide an automatic battery charging system that allows the vehicle to “plug in” to the charger by engaging stationary mounted charging plates. This engagement will be underneath the vehicle at known locations near the pickup stations. A photo of the charge shoe is shown below for illustration. The AGV will drive into a charging position by engaging the vehicle mounted charge shoe with a spring-loaded collector connected to the battery charger. The charger will sense the battery voltage and turn on charging the battery. There is no voltage present on the charge plate unless an AGV is actively charging.

In order to minimize the operational requirements, we have elected to supply sealed, maintenance-free batteries in lieu of standard flooded industrial batteries used on a typical forklift.
